Solid State Drives (SSD)


How they work:

There are 2 types of SSD gates, NAND and NOR. NAND gate SSDs are cheaper to make but are a bit slower than NOR gate SSDs

NAND gate SSDs use flash memory cells which work by "trapping" an electron in a floating gate, hence the name "flash memory".

NOR gate SSDs are called EEPROMS.

NOTE: nowadays both NOR and NAND SSDs use flash memory cells, but the book says only NAND uses them.



Advantages:

• Faster

• Lighter

• More reliable (no moving parts)

• Smaller

• Low power consumption


Disadvantages:

• High cost per GB

• Limited amount of write cycles




Optical media: CDs, DVDs and Blu-ray discs


How they work:

Optical drives use lasers to read and write data. The lasers burn tiny pits onto the surface of the disk whcih can later be read by a weaker laser (that will not change the shape of the disk).

Data is read by measuring the amount of light reflected back.

Optical media is usually used as a back-up system due to its durability but slow read speeds.


There are 3 main types of optical storage:


CDs:

CDs have the lasers with the biggest wavelengths (780nm), so they need more space to store the same amount of data.

They are usually used to store music as they cannot store anything larger like movies or games



DVDs:

DVDs have 650nm laser wavelengths, which is between the 2 other optical storage types

DVDs use a special technology called dual layering, which allows having twice as much data store in the same amount of space.

The laser can change focal length to read only 1 of the layers at a time, however birefringence (keyword) can occur which is when the focal length is between the 2 layers.

THis can cause light to refract into 2 separate beams which gives unreadable data.


Blu-Rays:

Blu-Rays have by far the smallest wavelengths (408nm) since they use blue light instead of red. They can store the most amount of data in the same amount of space.

They are used to store movies, games, etc.



Advantages:

• Durable

• Easy to transport


Disadvantages:

• Low storage capacity

• Slow read/write speeds

• Can be easily damaged
